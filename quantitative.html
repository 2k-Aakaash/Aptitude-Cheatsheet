Quantitative Aptitude Cheatsheet

Master the basics of quantitative aptitude with ease

Arithmetic Ability

Master basic arithmetic operations, percentages, ratios, and proportions.

Basic Arithmetic Operations

Understand and solve problems involving addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.

Example Problem:

Simplify: 25 + 36 - 12 × 2 ÷ 4

Solution:

25 + 36 - 6 = 55

Percentages

Calculate percentages, percentage changes, and solve problems involving percentages.

Example Problem:

What is 20% of 150?

Solution:

0.20 × 150 = 30

Ratios and Proportions

Solve problems involving ratios and proportions.

Example Problem:

If the ratio of apples to oranges is 3:2 and there are 30 apples, how many oranges are there?

Solution:

(30 / 3) × 2 = 20 oranges

Data Interpretation

Interpret data from tables, graphs, and charts to solve problems.

Tables

Read and analyze data presented in tables.

Example Problem:

Given the table of sales data, find the total sales for the year.

Month Sales
January 1000
February 1500
March 2000
Solution:

Total Sales = 1000 + 1500 + 2000 = 4500

Graphs

Analyze data presented in bar graphs, line graphs, and pie charts.

Example Problem:

Interpret the following bar graph to find the month with the highest sales.

Solution:

The month with the highest sales is March.
